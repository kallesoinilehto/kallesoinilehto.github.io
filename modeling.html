I have a lot of experience from 3D modeling. I've worked with both Blender and Rhino3D, modeling 3D prints and video game assets. The tablet you see here is the latest of my 3D models. During the project I learned to create UV maps by UV unwrapping objects. That way it was easy to move the materials from Blender to Unity. My modeling process has been heavily reliant on mesh primitives like cubes and cylinders. After creating a model with primitives, I use tools like bevel for edges and modifiers like boolean for editing objects.



One of my 3D print related projects was a conveyor belt (see the image below). Before beginning the modeling phase, I planned out a model that was simple enough for a successful print but still guaranteed a usable tool for work. After deciding the prototype, I began to model the conveyor belt in Rhino3D. After several days of honing the final version of the model, it was finally ready to be printed. The print needed a lot of material so the printing process took quite a while. I had to sand some parts of the model after printing but luckily the final product was a success.
